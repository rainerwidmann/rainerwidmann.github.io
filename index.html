About

I am a Data Scientist at Red Bull, where I work on analytical problems related to product quality and supplier management.

Previously, I worked as a Data Scientist at Zooplus, one of Europe's largest e-commerce companies for pet supplies. My work focused on experimentation and interpretable models of customer behavior, supporting decision making across customer retention, operational performance, promotions and supplier evaluation.

Before moving to industry, I was a researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Innovation and Competition, where I studied the economics of innovation and digital markets. I received my PhD in Managerial Economics and Strategy from Northwestern University.

Selected Industry Projects (E-Commerce)

Supplier Benchmarking and Customer Retention
Developed a causal benchmarking framework leveraging operational batch switches in logistics centers that generated quasi-random variation in supplier assignment to customers. The framework connects supplier-specific product experience to repeat-purchase behavior to evaluate supplier-specific product quality.
Pricing Experiment Analysis
Designed and analyzed pricing interventions using variation across products, countries, and time to evaluate the relative effectiveness of overfiller promotions (e.g. 20% more volume for free) and comparable price reductions on sales uplift in an e-commerce setting.
Operational Performance and Customer Value
Developed structural time-to-event models of customer repurchase behavior to quantify how delivery performance and product availability in e-commerce influence retention and long-term customer value.
Experimental Design for Pet Palatability Studies
Conducted power simulations and validation analyses for pet food palatability studies involving repeated-measures feeding experiments and multi-product choice settings to ensure robust identification of preference differences.
