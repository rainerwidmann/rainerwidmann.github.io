About

I am a Data Scientist at Zooplus, where I support decision making with interpretable models of customer behavior. My work focuses on applied econometrics, causal machine learning, and experimentation, helping to evaluate interventions, understand repurchase drivers, and inform strategy across product categories and customer segments.

Beginning in June 2026, I will join Red Bull as a Data Scientist.

Previously, I was a researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Innovation and Competition, where I studied the economics of innovation and digital markets. I received my PhD in Managerial Economics and Strategy from Northwestern University.

Selected Industry Projects

Supplier Benchmarking and Customer Retention
Developed a causal benchmarking framework leveraging operational batch switches in logistics centers that generated quasi-random variation in supplier assignment to customers. The framework connects supplier-specific product experience to repeat-purchase behavior to infer product quality.
Pricing Experiment Analysis
Designed and analyzed a pricing experiment to evaluate the relative effectiveness of overfiller promotions, such as 20% more volume for free, and comparable price reductions on sales uplift in an e-commerce setting.
Operational Performance and Customer Value
Built statistical models to quantify how delivery performance and product availability in e-commerce influence repurchase behavior and long-term customer value.
Experimental Design for Pet Palatability Studies
Conducted power simulations and validation analyses to ensure robust experimental designs for testing the palatability of pet food.
