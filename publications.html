Sexual Misconduct, Accused Scientists, and Their Research
with Marina Chugunova and Michael Rose
Review of Economics and Statistics, September (2025): 1–29
This paper studies whether the scientific community sanctions researchers involved in substantiated cases of sexual misconduct. Using citation and career outcomes, we show that accused scientists experience declines in citations and academic activity after allegations become public.

Motives, Gender, and Experience: Performance Effects in Crowdsourcing Contests
with Jonas Heite and Karin Hoisl
Strategy Science, September (2025)
This paper examines how contestants respond when facing stronger opponents in tournament-based crowdsourcing competitions. Using a quasi-experimental design based on Topcoder’s skill divisions, we show how motivation, gender, and experience shape performance responses to competitive pressure.

Immigrant Inventors and Local Income Taxes: Evidence from Swiss Municipalities
Journal of Public Economics, Issue 219 (2023): 104822
This paper studies how local income tax rates affect the residential choices of immigrant inventors in Switzerland. Exploiting sharp tax differences across state borders, I estimate how tax incentives influence the location choices of highly skilled innovators.

The Behavioral Additionality of Government Research Grants
International Journal of Industrial Organization, Volume 93 (2024): 103045
This paper examines how government research grants affect firms’ innovation behavior. Using data from the Austrian Research Promotion Agency, I show that grants increase patenting and encourage experienced firms to pursue more exploratory and unconventional innovation projects.

Cross-border Commuters and Knowledge Diffusion
with Gabriele Cristelli
Reject and Resubmit at Management Science
This paper studies how access to cross-border labor markets affects the diffusion of knowledge across regions. We examine a reform that expanded Swiss firms’ access to German cross-border commuters and show how labor mobility contributed to the use of German knowledge in Swiss innovation.
Working Paper
Relationship-specific Risks in Scientific Training and Advisor Hold-up Power
with Bruce Weinberg and Enrico Berkes
This paper studies the dependence of graduate students and postdocs on specific senior faculty relationships. Using advisor deaths as shocks, we examine how relationship-specific investments affect degree completion, research employment, and access to funding.
